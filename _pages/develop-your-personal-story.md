Your personal story provides the *emotion* that connects people to the logic of your presentation. It builds trust and shows you understand their struggles.

> **The Golden Rule:** Keep your story to **60 seconds or less**. The goal is to be relatable, not the hero.

## Step 1: Choose Your Angle

Select the angle that best fits your experience.

*   **The Former Client:** You started as a client, got a great result, and now you want to help others feel the same relief.
*   **The Career Changer:** You left a stressful or dead-end job for the freedom and impact this business provides.
*   **The Crusader:** You witnessed a financial tragedy (a death, an illness) that could have been prevented, and you're on a mission to stop it from happening to others.

> **Be Authentic:** Don't just copy the examples. Use your real experiences. Authenticity is what connects with people.

---

## Step 2: Write Your Story (Fill-in-the-Blanks)

Use this direct template to draft your story.

> "Let me quickly share **why** I do this.
>
> A few years ago, I was a **[Your Job/Situation]** and I was honestly struggling with **[Your Main Frustration, e.g., debt, feeling stuck, etc.]**.
>
> Then, someone showed me the Financial House concept, and it was a lightbulb moment. I finally realized that **[Your Key Realization, e.g., I wasn't broke, I was just uneducated]**.
>
> After I fixed my own situation, I knew I couldn't keep this a secret. There are millions of families as lost as I was, and that's why I'm so passionate about sharing this."

---

## Step 3: Refine and Practice

A good story isn't just written; it's performed.

1.  **Time Yourself:** Read it aloud. Use a stopwatch. If it's over 60 seconds, cut it down. Be ruthless.
2.  **Record and Review:** Record yourself on your phone. Does it sound authentic? Is it engaging?
3.  **Get Feedback:** Practice on 3 people (your upline, a friend, a partner). Ask them: "Did that feel real?" and "What did you take away from it?"

---

## Where to Use Your Story

Your story fits right after you explain the Financial House but **before** you explain compensation.

*   **Transition IN:** *"Now that you see the logic of the Financial House, let me quickly share why this is so personal to me..."*
*   **Transition OUT:** *"And because I'm so passionate about helping other families, I get paid in two ways..."*

## Quick Reference Checklist

- [ ] Is my story under 60 seconds?
- [ ] Does it hit all 3 beats (Before, Pivot, Mission)?
- [ ] Is the "Before" vulnerable and relatable?
- [ ] Is the "Pivot" a clear lightbulb moment?
- [ ] Is the "Mission" focused on helping others?
